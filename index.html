Introduction to Cyber Security - Cyber security has always been an important aspect of computing systems but its importance has increased greatly in recent years. The curriculum covers areas where cyber security is of major importance and the techniques used to secure computer systems. The areas looked at include computer operating systems (and increasingly, distributed operating systems), distributed applications (such as electronic commerce over the Internet) and embedded systems (ranging from smart cards to large industrial plant and telecommunications systems). Furthermore, the curriculum integrates the legal, ethical, and professional perspectives for instance to address concerns about data security, privacy, and societal impact of computing systems.
Software Project - Students, working individually, undertake a prescribed project that involves the development of a software system, aimed at solving a problem of practical significance. Academic staff will be involved in the supervision and monitoring of the project work.
Computer Systems - You will learn how to build a fully functioning stack of computer hardware and software, from the ground up. That means starting only from a single primitive component, the Nand gate. Informally, the entire journey is called "Nand to Tetris", since at the end your computer will be able to run a Tetris game.
